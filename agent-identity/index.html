Building Trust in the Age of AI

Setting up identity for a new kind of actor — the AI agent.

The Problem

A New Kind of Actor

Twilio has identity tools for humans with point solutions to verify a user is who they say they are. But there's no way to safely authenticate or authorize the AI agents now acting alongside them.

For Example

Imagine you are a financial tool called Owl Trade. End users of your product want to connect ChatGPT to make trades on their behalf. How do you do this securely?

Overview

From point solution to full identity platform

Expanding to a full Identity platform gives AI agents a safe way to be authenticated and, with a person's consent, act on their behalf.

Meet Customers Where They Are

Most customers already have their own system for managing users. Twilio doesn't need to replace it — we just need a way to trust the identity it hands over.

Step 1 · Verify Identity

Connect, Don't Rebuild

Owl Trade already authenticates its users — that doesn't change. All Owl Trade needs is a way to hand that identity off to an agent, the moment a user wants to connect one.

Step 1 · Verify Identity

A File, Not Another Input Field

PEM keys are exact — a stray line break or smart quote from a copy-paste can silently corrupt one, and the failure is nearly impossible to debug. So Owl Trade uploads a file instead of pasting text into a box.

Step 2 · Connect the Agent

Asking Permission

Once a user's identity is verified, Owl Trade can ask if they want to connect an agent. For Owl Trade, that means prompting the user to authorize the agent.

Step 3 · Store & Manage

Who's Connected?

These are agents with live access to Owl Trade's product — worth monitoring like any other integration. If Owl Trade ever decides an agent shouldn't have access anymore, they can remove it.

Approvals Live Here Too

Step 3 · Store & Manage

Down to One User

The Clients table answers an aggregate question — what can an agent do across all of Owl Trade's users. Identities answers the opposite: for one specific trader, exactly what they've granted — down to a per-user, per-agent level.

Identities → Authorized Clients

Reversible by Design

Removing an agent from Owl Trade entirely is immediate and irreversible, cutting off every trader at once. But if a single trader decides they no longer want that agent acting for them, that's designed differently — reversible, just a re-grant prompt at their next sign-in.

Step 4 · Loop the User Back In

A Deliberate Pause

Agents are valuable because they can act autonomously — placing routine trades without a human clicking through each one.

But an actual trade going through is consequential enough that a trader might want to be pulled back in first. How much friction that takes should scale with what's actually at stake.

Step 4 · Loop the User Back In

First Time, Then Fast

Owl Trade has no existing Verify service yet, so it's guided through a dedicated wizard — scoped to just what this needs, since the step reaches into a different Twilio product. Once that service exists, every return visit collapses into a single side-modal.
